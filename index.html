📅 Month 1 — The beginning
I finally said what I’ve been feeling… and you said yes.
Neither of us planned it. But somehow, it became everything.

📅 Month 3 — Getting real
Somewhere along the way, liking turned into something deeper.
Time disappeared and neither of us noticed.

It didn’t happen all at once. Little by little, we started trusting each other more, caring more, choosing each other without even thinking about it. What we had stopped feeling new—and started feeling real.

📅 Month 6 — Halfway to today
The First Rough Patch
Things got harder than either of us expected.

This is where everything changed. Trust was shaken, things hurt, and it started falling apart. But even then, we had to decide what we meant to each other.

📅 Month 11 — The Unimaginable
The Break Up
We fell apart… but somehow, we didn’t let go.

We broke up, and for a while it felt like everything was over. Nothing was the same, and we weren’t even official anymore. But even through all of that, we didn’t stop caring, didn’t stop choosing each other in our own way. Somehow, we stayed.

📅 Month 15 — Right now
Today
15 months and we’re still here… and that has to mean something.

We’re still a little hurt, still figuring things out—but we’re still here. After everything, we didn’t walk away for good. We’re still choosing each other, just more carefully this time. Maybe not perfect, maybe not easy—but real. And this time, we know what we have to protect.

My love,

Fifteen months sounds like a measurement. A unit of time. But it's not, really. It's a collection of Tuesday evenings and Saturday laughter. Of inside jokes that would make no sense to anyone else. Of the specific way you roll your eyes right before you admit I was right about something.

I want you to know: I don't take any of it for granted. Not the easy days, and especially not the hard ones — because those are the ones that showed me what we're actually made of. And we're made of something good.

You've made me braver than I was before you. More patient. More present. You've quietly, stubbornly, gently made me a better person — and I don't think you even know you've been doing it.

Here's what I know, fifteen months in: I want fifteen more. And then fifteen after that. Not because I have to — but because of all the lives I could be living, this one, with you in it, is the only one I want.

Thank you for letting me love you. Thank you for loving me back.

Always yours ♥
